For this recipe you will need:

  • 2-3 Carrots
  • 2-3 sticks of Celery
  • 1-2 large Onions
  • 3-5 garlic cloves
  • 2 fresh Chilli's
  • 750g Beef mince
  • 500g pork mince
  • A pack of smoked bacon rashers
  • fresh parmesan
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 tins of Good quality chopped tomatoes
  • A big spoonful of tomatoe paste
  • half a bottle of Red wine
  • 2-3 Dried bay leaves
  • a handful of Rosemary and thyme
  • Salt and pepper
  • A lot Fresh basil

So you have the ingredients and now it's time to cook! Keep in mind this recipe is an all day recipe, be prepared to be here a while!!

Step one

  1. finely dice your onion, carrot and celery to create your Mirepoix.This should be roughly 2 parts onion, 1 part carrot, 1 part celery. after you have diced them set aside, then chop your chillis and garlic, and finely finely dice your bacon and set aside. Then into a hot pan add a glug of extra virgin olive oil and add your Mirepoix. and chilli, keep at a medium heat until fragrant and translucent, then add your garlic in and turn off the heat.

Step 2

  1. Empty your pot into a blender and add a glug of olive oil, blend until smooth. Add the now smooth paste into the pan to cook until golden brown, optionally you run the paste through a strainer to get rid of any fibers from the celery.(this can and will affect the final products consistancy.) having just used the blender add the chopped tomatoes to it and blend them also for a smooth consistancy.

Step 3

  1. In a seperate pan fry off your beef, bacon and pork mince, add a pinch of salt when you fry them. fry until dark brown then add a spoonful of tomatoe paste to slightly brown before adding the meat into the Mirepoix. once added stir and add your half a bottle of red wine, cook until it reduces to a sryup.

step 4

  1. Once reduced add your tomatoes, salt to taste, a lot of black pepper, rosemary and thyme, the rind of your parmesan and your bay leaves. Put a lid on your pot and leave to cook on a low heat, come back every 20 minutes or so to stir. after a few hours or when has reduced greatly add in a bunch of basil and serve!
